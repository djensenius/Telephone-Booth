# Artist statement

**Title:** Telephone Booth

**Materials:** Rotary pay phone, phone booth, custom electronics, sound.
Site-specific questions composed for St. Anne's Anglican Church, Toronto.

Telephone Booth is an instrument of confession and chance communion. A rotary
pay phone, that vanishing fixture of shared public life, waits where it does
not belong. Its plate promises free calls: dial 1 to leave a message, dial 2 to
retrieve one. Yet the phone answers with a question you did not ask, and returns
a stranger's reply severed from its prompt. Voices circulate and recombine,
uncoupled from their authors, until the booth speaks in a chorus no single
person composed.

Here, the work sits within St. Anne's, a sanctuary transfigured by fire and now
becoming something else. Its dome and painted heavens are gone, its walls hold
open sky, its congregation gathers in a new shape. The questions are drawn from
this place, from ash and the stubborn will to rebuild. To lift the receiver here
is to speak into the space between what was and what is arriving, and to receive,
without context, the testimony of someone you will never meet.

What accumulates is an assembly of the honest fragments passersby are willing to
give.

## Artist bio

David Jensenius is a composer and media artist whose work moves between sound,
performance, installation, and public space. Working with collage, phonography,
electronics, and custom software, he builds systems that listen back, writing the
code that turns obsolete technologies and everyday encounters into sites of
chance, intimacy, and emergent dialogue. Through playful transformations of
conceptual and material systems, he develops problems worth having, engaging
across scales and complexities both human and nonhuman. Jensenius performs and
exhibits his work across Canada and internationally.
